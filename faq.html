FAQ Some commonly asked questions...

  Where is Camp YouCan located?

Camp YouCan will be held at YMCA Camp Kitaki grounds.

  How do we get to Camp Kitaki campgrounds?

Camp Kitaki is located between Omaha & Lincoln. The fastest & easiest way to get there is to take I-80 and take Mahoney State Park exit 426. Go south on HWY 66 approximately 4 miles until you pass through the town of South Bend. Camp is just past South Bend, the entrance is on the left hand side of the road.

  Is transportation to and from the camp provided?

No, there is no sponsored transportation. Parents are responsible for taking campers to and picking campers up from Camp YouCan.

  What time is camper check-in?

Campers can check-in at Camp YouCan from 4:00-5:30pm on Friday, Sept. 5th.

  What is the camper check-in process?

Parents must accompany their child to check-in. At that time, camp medication schedule/emergency information form must be turned in to nursing staff along with all medications that will need to be given during the camp day. You then will have the opportunity to meet the camp staff and the camper’s counselors.

  What time is camper check-out?

Families are welcome to join us at Camp YouCan at 1:00pm. You will have a great opportunity to be shown around the campgrounds by your child or to be taken to their favorite activity of the day. At 12:00pm all families are invited to have dinner followed by s’mores and songs at the closing campfire.

  How many counselors will be at Camp YouCan?

Camp YouCan will try to keep to a ratio or 1 counselor for every 3-4 campers. Camp YouCan will also have 2 camp directors, various other volunteers, and at least two nurses available at all times for our campers.

  What qualifications and training do the counselors have?

The camp staff will include, among others, nurses, school counselors/teachers, and other professionals. All counselors will be at least 21 years of age. All of the Camp YouCan staff has been trained in seizure recognition and what to do if a seizure occurs. YMCA Camp Kitaki professionally trained staff will run each activity.

  What if a camper does not want to take part in a particular activity?

Although campers are encouraged to try all activities, no camper has to do any activities he or she does not want to do.

  What contact will the parents and children have during camp?

Camp YouCan does not encourage parents and children to talk on the phone during camp as this may foster homesickness. In case of an emergency or to inquire about your camper, a phone number will be given to you at camper registration to reach camp staff if needed.

  How should I package my child’s medications to bring to camp?

. Please bring all medications in their original containers. The bottle must state the child’s name, name of the medication, dosage and time taken. A prescribing doctor’s name and contact information must be on the bottle.

If your child has a VNS, please send the magnet along with them to camp and include any instructions. The medication in the original prescription bottles and any other medical supplies MUST be placed all-together in one zip-lock storage bag with your child’s first and last name (written in permanent marker) on the outside of the bag.

*If the medication is not stated on the Health Examination and Medication Order Form or additional permission is not stated in written documentation then we cannot administer the medication.

  How and by whom will seizures be charted?

Seizures will be charted by whomever observes the seizure whether it be a counselor or nurse. The counselor will maintain the log of seizure activity. If parents want a copy of the seizure log, please request it upon picking your child up.

  Will parents be notified if emergency medication is administered?

Parents will be notified of any unusual seizure activity (i.e. greater frequency than noted in camper’s application) and of any emergency care administered.

  What should my child bring to camp?

Comfortable clothing and tennis shoes are highly advised. Dress appropriately for the weather keeping in mind that camp concludes in the evening when the weather usually cools down. Sunglasses, water bottle, and sunscreen are also encouraged. - Please DO NOT send: money, knives/firearms, food, iPods, electrical games, CD’s, cell phones, or anything of significant value.

  What is the registration process?

Follow the registration process on-line. Application forms must be turned in by August 4th, 2014.
