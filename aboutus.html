OUR MISSION that we live by...

The MISSION of Camp YouCan is to provide opportunities for children and teens with epilepsy to strengthen self-confidence, develop friendships, and explore independence.

FOUNDERS and why they started Camp YouCan



Meg Busing, a Registered Nurse, created Camp YouCan in 2011 following a 10 year battle with intractable epilepsy and brain surgery. Meg was in a car accident in highschool and acquired a life altering Traumatic Brain Injury. Meg made a successful recover after physical, occupational, and speech therapy and graduated from highschool. Five years later, a seizure during a presentation in nursing school, would kick start a 10 year battle with epilepsy. Meg felt not only the physical aspects of epilepsy- she also experienced the emotional sides of epilepsy that are rarely discussed. Embarrassment, feelings of isolation, frustration, anxiety, fear, and lack of understanding amongst peers and the limits put upon a person with epilepsy really affected her. It was after that 2011 brain surgery to treat the seizures that Meg and her husband, Kael Busing, decided to pursue a goal to create Nebraska and Iowaâ€™s first camp for kids with epilepsy.

The camp is titled Camp YouCan because so many times with epilepsy, a person is reminded of things that they cannot and should not do. But at Camp YouCan, kids will be given encouragement to try new things that they may not always get the opportunity to do; all while being surrounded by other campers who can truly understand the physical and emotional challenges that go along with epilepsy. A day of archery, boating, a ropes course, zip line, fishing, arts and crafts and team building activities will be awesome, but even more so, Camp YouCan provides campers ages 7-18 a day full of chances to build self- confidence, to discover independence, and to see all that they CAN do.
